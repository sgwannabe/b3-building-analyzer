9. RISKS & RED FLAGS

Floor-Level Sensitivity: Lower floor units (1st–3rd) trade at significant discounts and suffer from Liquidity Shift. Unit 1A took 315 days to sell, while upper floor units move in under 30 days. Buyers of lower-floor units must price in a 100+ day exit timeline and potential 10%+ ask-to-close compression.
Renovation Premium Distortion: The PPSF spread from $1,800 to $4,600 is extreme. Some units are "gut renovated" while others may be "estate condition." The 12F off-market at $4,600 PPSF almost certainly reflects a full renovation, not organic building appreciation. Buyers must adjust comps for condition, not just line — a failure to do so risks overpaying by 30%+.
B-Line Structural Lag: The B-line (2-Bed) consistently underperforms. Unit 10B's 1.5% CAGR over 8 years is a genuine red flag. Investors in B-line units are holding a fundamentally different asset than E-line owners — one that preserves capital but does not grow it meaningfully.
Missing Data — HOA & Taxes: This analysis does not include common charges or property tax burden. For a 1931 pre-war building, maintenance costs can be material ($1.50–$2.50/SF/month). Effective yield after carrying costs may be significantly lower than the raw rent capture suggests. Investors should request the building's financials before committing.
